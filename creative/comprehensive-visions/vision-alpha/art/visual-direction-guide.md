## Pixel Art Specifications

### Resolution & Canvas Sizes

**Game Resolution**: 1920x1080 (16:9) - Scaled pixel art
**Base Pixel Size**: 16x16 tiles (world grid)
**Pixel Ratio**: 3-4x scale for crisp modern displays

**Character Sprite Sizes:**
- **Player Character**: 32x32 pixels (2 tiles tall)
- **Small Enemies**: 16x16 to 24x24
- **Medium Enemies**: 32x32 to 48x48
- **Large Enemies**: 64x64 to 96x96
- **Bosses**: 128x128 to 256x256
- **NPCs**: 24x32 (slightly taller than player)

**Environment Tilesets:**
- **Base Tiles**: 16x16 (walls, floors)
- **Props**: Varied sizes (16x16 to 64x64)
- **Backgrounds**: Multi-layer parallax (3-5 layers)

**UI Elements:**
- **Icons**: 16x16 or 32x32
- **Portraits**: 64x64 or 96x96
- **Font**: 8x8 base pixel font (custom musical theme)

## Art Style Characteristics

### Line Work & Detail Level

**Approach**: **Semi-detailed Pixel Art** (between minimalist and hyper-detailed)

**Guidelines:**
- Clear silhouettes (readable from distance)
- Selective detail (focus on important features)
- Clean outlines (1-2 pixel black outlines for characters)
- Anti-aliasing on curves (smooth, not jagged)
- Dithering sparingly (texture, not noise)

**Detail Priority:**
1. **Character faces/eyes** - Maximum expression
2. **Musical instruments** - Recognizable, detailed
3. **Rhythm indicators** - Crystal clear
4. **Background atmosphere** - Soft, not distracting

**Inspiration**: Eastward's detail balance - rich but not overwhelming

## Animation Philosophy

### Frame Counts & Timing

**Player Character Animations:**
- **Idle**: 6-8 frames (breathing, subtle movement)
- **Walk**: 8 frames (smooth gait)
- **Run**: 6 frames (dynamic, fast)
- **Jump**: 4 frames (anticipation, apex, descent, land)
- **Attack Combo**: 6-10 frames per attack
- **Special Moves**: 12-16 frames (more dramatic)
- **Dodge Roll**: 6 frames (i-frames clearly visible)
- **Hurt**: 3 frames (hit react)
- **Death**: 8-12 frames (dramatic)

**Enemy Animations:**
- **Idle**: 4-6 frames (vary per enemy type)
- **Move**: 4-8 frames
- **Attack**: 6-10 frames (with telegraph)
- **Death**: 6-8 frames (particle effects)

**Environmental Animations:**
- **Water**: 4 frames (gentle wave)
- **Fire**: 6 frames (flickering)
- **Grass**: 3 frames (breeze)
- **Flags**: 6 frames (wind flow)

**Musical Animations:**
- **Beat Pulse**: 4 frames (world pulses with music)
- **Note Particles**: 8 frames (float and fade)
- **Rhythm Indicators**: Smooth scaling/color shifts

### Animation Principles

**12 Principles Applied to Pixel Art:**
1. **Squash & Stretch**: Subtle (1-2 pixels)
2. **Anticipation**: Clear telegraph frames
3. **Staging**: Readable silhouette always
4. **Follow Through**: Hair, clothes, effects trail
5. **Ease In/Out**: Smooth starts/stops
6. **Arcs**: Natural movement paths
7. **Secondary Action**: Breathing during idle
8. **Timing**: Match music beats (critical!)
9. **Exaggeration**: Slightly cartoony for readability
10. **Appeal**: Every character likeable/interesting

**Rhythm-Specific Animation:**
- All major animations have **beat-synced versions**
- Attacks have **on-beat** vs **off-beat** visual differences
- Perfect timing = **extra visual flourish** (flash, trail, particles)
- Combo chains = **escalating visual intensity**

## Color Palette Strategy

### Master Color Approach

**Philosophy**: **Vibrant but Controlled**
- Rich, saturated colors for musical energy
- Distinct palettes per biome/genre
- High contrast for readability
- Limited palette per asset (8-12 colors typically)

### Hue-Shifting Technique
Following pixel art color theory:
- **Highlights**: Shift hue toward warm (yellow/orange)
- **Shadows**: Shift hue toward cool (blue/purple)
- **Avoid pure black/white**: Always tinted
- **Color ramps**: 4-6 shades per color (light to dark)

### Color Communication
Colors convey meaning:
- **Red**: Danger, beats 1 & 3, power, aggression
- **Blue**: Calm, beats 2 & 4, defense, water
- **Yellow**: Energy, perfect timing, electricity
- **Green**: Nature, healing, growth
- **Purple**: Mystery, advanced techniques, magic
- **Orange**: Heat, rhythm intensity, combo building

## Environmental Art

### Tileset Design

**Modular System:**
- 16x16 base tiles for efficiency
- Auto-tiling support (edges, corners)
- Variations to avoid repetition (3-5 versions per tile)
- Biome-specific tilesets

**Detail Levels:**
- **Foreground**: High detail (player interacts here)
- **Midground**: Medium detail (main play area)
- **Background**: Soft detail (atmosphere only)

### Parallax Background Layers

**5-Layer System:**
1. **Sky Layer**: Slowest scroll (clouds, sun/moon)
2. **Far Background**: Mountains, distant buildings
3. **Mid Background**: Trees, structures
4. **Near Background**: Close environmental details
5. **Foreground**: Player layer (main gameplay)

**Rhythm Integration:**
- Layers pulse subtly with beat
- Color shifts with music intensity
- Particles float on rhythm

### Lighting & Atmosphere

**Modern Techniques in Pixel Art:**
- **Dynamic Lighting**: Godot shaders for real-time light
- **Glow Effects**: Rim lighting on characters
- **Shadows**: Simple but effective (ellipse under characters)
- **Weather**: Rain, snow, fog effects
- **Time of Day**: Color palette shifts (morning/day/evening/night)

**Inspiration**: Sea of Stars' modern pixel art lighting

### Interactive Elements

**Visual Feedback:**
- Doors: Glow when accessible
- Chests: Sparkle before opening
- Switches: Clear on/off states
- Rhythm Objects: Pulse with beat
- Destructibles: Crack before breaking

## Aseprite Workflow

### Project Organization

**File Structure:**
```
/assets/sprites/
  /characters/
    /player/
      player_idle.aseprite
      player_walk.aseprite
      player_attack.aseprite
    /npcs/
    /enemies/
  /environments/
    /forest/
    /desert/
    /etc/
  /items/
  /ui/
  /vfx/
```

**Layer Organization:**
- **Outline Layer**: Character borders (always on top)
- **Color Layer**: Main colors
- **Shading Layer**: Multiply mode for shadows
- **Highlights Layer**: Add mode for lights
- **Details Layer**: Eyes, small features
- **Effects Layer**: Optional glows, etc.

### Animation Timeline

**Frame Tagging:**
- Tag each animation (idle, walk, attack1, etc.)
- Set loop/ping-pong settings
- Note frame durations

**Onion Skinning:**
- 2 frames before/after visible
- Smooth animation flow
- Avoid jarring transitions

### Export Settings

**For Godot 4.5:**
- Export as sprite sheet (PNG)
- Transparent background
- No padding between frames (Godot handles this)
- JSON data for frame positions (optional)

**Naming Convention:**
```
charactername_animationname_###.png
Example: player_walk_001.png
```

## Technical Specifications

### Godot 4.5 Integration

**Sprite Import:**
- Texture Filter: Nearest (pixel-perfect)
- Mipmaps: Disabled
- VRAM Compression: Disabled (keep crisp)

**AnimatedSprite2D:**
- SpriteFrames resource
- Frame rate: Match animation (usually 8-12 FPS for pixel art)
- Autoplay for idles

**Shaders for Pixel Art:**
- Palette swapping (color customization)
- Glow/outline shaders
- Hit flash effects (modulate color briefly)
- Rhythm pulse (scale subtly on beat)

### Performance Optimization

**Atlasing:**
- Combine sprites into texture atlases
- Reduce draw calls
- Automatic in Godot 4.5

**Culling:**
- Only animate visible sprites
- Disable off-screen animations

**LOD (Level of Detail):**
- Simplified animations at distance
- Reduce particle counts off-screen
