# Game-by-Game Breakdown: Action RPGs & Roguelikes

**Research Focus:** Combat systems, build diversity, progression mechanics, and replayability hooks from 20+ influential action RPGs and roguelikes (1990-2025)

**Purpose:** Extract actionable ideas for our Rhythm RPG project

---

## 1. Hades (2020) - Supergiant Games

### Combat System
- **Feel:** Fluid, responsive action with generous dodge roll i-frames
- **Impact frames & particle explosions:** Every hit feels significant
- **Boon system:** Gods grant temporary powers that stack and synergize
- **Weapon variety:** 6 weapons (sword, spear, shield, bow, fists, gun), each with multiple aspects

### Build Variety
- **Boon synergies:** Categorized by frequency (high-use vs low-use attacks)
- **Duo boons:** Powerful combinations requiring boons from 2+ gods
- **Weapon aspects:** Each weapon has 4 different playstyles
- **4 gods per run:** Limited pool creates strategic choices

### Meta-Progression
- **Mirror of Night:** Permanent stat upgrades with alternative options
- **Relationship progression:** Death advances NPC stories and unlocks dialogue
- **Weapon upgrades:** Spend resources between runs
- **House contractor:** Unlock new items, cosmetics, and quality of life features

### Replayability Hooks
- **Death as narrative progress:** Every death reveals more story
- **Emerging relationships:** Romance and friendship deepen over runs
- **Heat system:** 40+ difficulty modifiers for endgame
- **Prophecies:** Long-term goals across multiple runs

### What We Can Adapt for Rhythm RPG
1. **Musical "Boons":** Gods/Spirits grant rhythm-based power-ups (tempo changes, beat modifiers)
2. **Perfect timing bonuses:** Land attacks on-beat for boon effects
3. **NPC relationships through music:** Jam sessions, duets advance stories
4. **Death advances musical lore:** Each failure teaches new songs, unlocks instruments
5. **Heat-style difficulty:** Add rhythm complexity (polyrhythms, time signatures)
6. **Weapon aspects as music genres:** Same weapon plays jazz vs metal vs classical

## 2. Dead Cells (2018) - Motion Twin

### Combat System
- **Feel:** Extremely fluid, combo-focused with dodge canceling
- **Dodge roll:** I-frames allow aggressive play through enemy attacks
- **Weapon variety:** Swords, bows, shields, placeable traps
- **Brutality/Tactics/Survival:** Color-coded builds affect damage scaling

### Build Variety
- **Mutations:** 3 passive upgrades per run, unlocked permanently
- **Weapon/mutation synergies:** Color matching for optimal damage
- **Playstyle diversity:** Melee rushdown, ranged tactical, trap specialist
- **Dynamic adaptation:** Builds emerge from what you find

### Progression
- **Permanent unlocks:** Blueprints unlock new weapons/mutations
- **Boss Cells (BC):** 5 difficulty tiers with new mechanics per tier
- **Cells currency:** Spend between biomes, lose on death
- **Biome branching:** Multiple paths through game world

### Replayability
- **BC difficulty tiers:** Fundamentally change enemy behavior
- **Daily challenges:** Compete on leaderboards
- **Biome variety:** 12+ biomes with unique enemies/aesthetics
- **Weapon discovery:** 190+ weapons with unique movesets

### What We Can Adapt for Rhythm RPG
1. **Color-coded rhythm builds:** Beat intensity (heavy/fast/technical) affects damage
2. **Mutation-style rhythm perks:** "Perfect beats spawn projectiles," "Combo counter = damage multiplier"
3. **Difficulty tiers add rhythm complexity:** BC1 = 16th notes, BC2 = polyrhythms, etc.
4. **Biome musical themes:** Jazz swamp, metal volcano, classical cathedral
5. **Combo flow with rhythm:** Chain attacks must match beat patterns
6. **Trap placement on rhythm grid:** Set traps on specific beats

## 7. Transistor (2014) - Supergiant Games

### Combat System
- **Turn() planning mode:** Freeze time, plan actions, execute
- **Real-time option:** Can fight without using Turn()

- **4 active + 12 modifier slots:** Deep customization

### Strategic Depth
- **Turn() vs real-time balance:** Choose when to plan vs react
- **Function combinations:** Exponential possibilities from 16 functions
- **Positioning importance:** Plan movement carefully
- **Prediction system:** See attack outcomes before executing

### Aesthetic Integration
- **Music reactive:** Soundtrack shifts with gameplay
- **Humming protagonist:** Red hums along to music
- **Limiter challenges:** Difficulty modifiers for testing
- **Recursive mode:** NG+ with remixed encounters

### What We Can Adapt for Rhythm RPG
1. **"Rhythm Mode" vs real-time:** Freeze to plan beat sequence, then execute
2. **Function-style ability system:** Beat patterns usable as attack/modifier/passive
3. **16-slot rhythm deck:** Curate perfect moveset from larger pool
4. **Planning beats ahead:** Queue 10 seconds of inputs, watch them execute
5. **Recovery mechanic:** Miss too many beats = that ability locks temporarily
6. **Limiter-style rhythm challenges:** "Only use off-beats," "No repeated notes"
